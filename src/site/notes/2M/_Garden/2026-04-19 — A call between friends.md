# A call between friends

### *On finding peace, building thinking teams, and the next beautiful step*

## What this conversation is about

Minh Thu had just returned from three days in the mountains — a trip she called *the light child tracking*. She came back changed. Not in the way people say they are changed, but in the quieter way: she found a room inside herself that had always been there, and she finally sat down in it.

This call is Nhan asking her to describe what she found. It becomes something else: two people building a shared language for what it means to pay attention — to trust the next small step, to let peace arrive without forcing it. Somewhere in the middle, Nhan describes Mementori — the thinking system he is building — and something clicks between the two ideas. *Inner peace and outer intelligence. Both asking the same question: what is the simplest next step from here?*

---

## The moments that mattered

- **The trip was not a movie.** She refused the frame. *"Do it. Then you will feel it. Because you never can see it for me."* The experience was irreducibly personal — she had not brought a camera to perform it.

- **She discovered the breathing room.** Three days of not judging anything she heard, saw, or touched. *"When I listen, it's easy to say — the sound of the mountain is like this and that. I stopped all of that. I just listen."* She slept through a windy night while her friend could not. Peace arrived not from silence, but from not needing it.

- **Going with strangers freed her.** No need to be a good person for them. No performance, no reputation to protect. The peace was unperformed — and deeper for it.

- **She recognised the Buddha level.** At a moment of stillness on the mountain: *"When Buddha — if I can describe that — he's a recogniser. He knows, he recognises, he understands. He reached Buddha level in the game of life."* She felt it. For the first time.

- **The slip that taught her.** Nearly fell crossing a waterfall stone. Legs exhausted, delayed half a second. She recovered. And understood: *"It's not only what you can see — but what is possible."*

- **The sentence she carried home.** Nhan's question — *what is the next most beautiful, simple step?* — traveled with her through every hard moment on the trail. The step was the key to the stone, and to everything else.

- **She chose not to send notes to the group.** In the trip she almost recorded reflections to share with the group of participants. Then she deleted the idea. *"What I got from this trip is just so simple — and I would keep doing this in the future."* Some things are not for broadcasting.

- **Meditation for the very active.** Ten-day silent retreats feel too forced for her. Movement + nature + breathing together — that is her entry door. She plans to try a few quiet minutes daily, building from the feeling she found.

- **Mementori and the Dream Team.** Nhan described how this recording travels into a box and is opened by an entire thinking team: Laila, Claude, Einstein, Steve Jobs, Elon Musk. Kaizen 3 rounds — each member gives their view, guest speakers arrive, the team spirals upward together. *"You get the perfect consultants. How many perspectives do you want for the same situation?"*

- **Add Buddha to the Dream Team.** Nhan, mid-sentence: *"Guys — please add Buddha to hashtag Dream Team. In all Kaizen 3 rounds. Hashtag Kaizen 3."* Noted.

- **Minh Thu as a fast decision-maker.** She reads cases at speed, marks what matters instantly. She does not need someone to read for her — she needs a mirror that replicates her judgment. *"If you give it the reading time of an evening — to read all the ways you solve cases — it would replicate your decision making."*

- **The AI assistant question.** Nhan asked her: if you had one assistant, what are your top three? She could not answer the question as asked. Her thinking process is too fast, too instinctive. The assistant would need to already know her brand, her cases, what to mark — and learn it in one week.

- **The chess invitation.** Nhan offered to set up an asynchronous chess game between Minh Thu and her teacher Mrs. Xibong — using the same app he uses with Mr. Tristan in Germany. Move whenever you find the time. A small, unhurried gift.

- **E-bikes and range.** A brief detour into e-bike shopping: suspension, big wheels for terrain, battery range for the Da Nang mountains. *"Madame Hans can go to Hoi An and back — 50 km in one charge."* They talked about what the next trip would need.

- **One button to delete everything.** Nhan told her directly: *"If you choose to delete this audio recording and the transcription and everything that is connected to it — it's basically one button. And this choice will be always there."* She heard it. It mattered.

- **WBD — Will Be Done.** The phrase that ends Nhan's instructions to the team. Not a command. A confirmation. The team takes care of it and returns with results.

---

## Where this leads

Minh Thu does not need many tools. She is fast, decisive, already reads cases at speed. What she needs is a mirror for the parts she already trusts in herself — something that can *replicate* her judgment, not replace it. She is beginning to see how that might be possible.

For Nhan, this call is confirmation: the most important entry point to any thinking system is not commands or prompts. It is *breathing*. It is the pause before the next step. The entire architecture of Mementori rests on that.

They parted warmly. The mountain is still there. The breathing room is still there. So is this.

## The conversation

*Transcribed by Groq Whisper Large v3 Turbo · Vietnamese + English · 48 minutes*

---

**Nhan** — *opening*

> *"The stage is yours, Mrs. Mintu — on your experience of the light child tracking. The perspectives you took from that experience. And what remains challenging."*

#NHN its not about “light child” but about Lai Chau, the northern trekking region we went together, near Sapa. #WBD

---

**On describing the trip as a movie**

Nhan asked for a trailer. A genre. A name. Something watchable.

Minh Thu resisted. She had live photos from the trip. Nhan is in most of them.

*"Yeah — that's a bit sad."*

Laughter. Then she said the true thing:

> *"It's very personal experience that I never can see — people seeing that. I look into that, or make it a movie, or even look at myself. I am more with me, totally."*

*"Do it. Then you will feel it. Because you never can see it for me."*

---

**On finding peace**

Her intention going in: she had always been too active — *"too much, a lot of things."* Ten-day silent retreats felt impossible for someone like her. She wanted to find peace through movement, not stillness by force.

What she found:

> *"Every distraction — I would stop. Even when I touch the tree I try not to judge. Even I look around I try not to think. All is just feeling, listen."*

She slept through the night the wind howled. Her friend could not.

> *"Inside me it's very peaceful. The kids talking around — I talk to them or not. And then — I don't care, because I don't mind. Later I will meet them."*

The advantage of traveling with strangers: you don't need to perform. The peace is unperformed — and deeper for it.

---

**On the near-fall at the waterfall**

She wanted to jump to a stone. Three days of exhaustion in her legs — they delayed half a second behind her brain's instruction. She crashed forward. She recovered.

> *"I saw not only what you can see — but what is possible. And the possible is basically the joke. Because before three days, someone would tell me this is possible to do. Yes, of course. And then I would go there the first day and want to stop: what the fuck am I doing here? It's so inconvenient. I had to sleep and it smells like dogs."*

Then the breathing came. And the breathing became the entry door to everything else.

> *"It's basically: sit down, not to meditate. But sit down to breathe."*

---

**On the sentence**

Nhan's question — *what is the next most beautiful, simple step?* — traveled with her for all three days.

> *"Don't think about the top of the mountain — you will get there. Don't forget why you started. The next most simple step there."*

She learned it from the women who had done this a thousand times before her. She did it for the first time. The step was the key to the stone.

---

**On Mementori and Kaizen 3**

> *"I just send it to a box — and that box will be opened by the entire AI team I created. There's Laila, there's Claude, there's Opus, there's Einstein, there's Steve Jobs, there's Elon Musk."*

He stopped mid-sentence.

> *"Guys — please add Buddha to hashtag Dream Team. In all Kaizen 3 rounds."*

Three rounds: each member gives their perspective. Guest speakers arrive, recommended by the team from their own knowledge. The members speak across each other — not to fight, but to spiral upward. Elon Musk arrives in round three, for execution. Then together: the golden path.

> *"You get the perfect consultants. How many perspectives do you want for the same situation?"*

Minh Thu heard this and immediately understood. She asked: what if I had one assistant?

Then two. Then a hundred.

> Minh Thu: *"You can save me a lot of time doing that."*

---

**On deletion**

Near the end, plainly:

> *"If you choose to delete this audio recording and the transcription and everything connected to it — it's one button. And this choice will always be there."*

She heard it. She did not ask questions about it. Some things are understood without discussion.

---

**Closing**

*End of April. Between Mrs. Minh Thu (Miss Minh Thu, Mrs. Ye Pluk) and An Xiu Nyang. Peace.*
